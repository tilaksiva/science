👋 Meet Splash!

Hi friend! I'm Splash, a tiny drop of water. I live in the big ocean. But I'm not going to stay here forever... I'm about to go on an incredible journey — up into the sky, across the land, and back home again! Want to come with me?

☀️

1️⃣ Evaporation

The Sun is shining down on the ocean. Tap the Sun a few times to heat up the water — watch what happens to Splash!

💭 What do you notice? Splash turned invisible and floated UP! What do you think happened to Splash?

2️⃣ Condensation

High in the sky it's cold ❄️. The invisible water vapor cools down and huddles together into tiny droplets — that's how a cloud is born! Millions of tiny droplets join together, just like Splash is about to.

🎤 Try this: gently blow into your microphone like the wind, to help more droplets join the cloud! (Or just tap the button.)

3️⃣ Precipitation

The cloud is now so full and heavy that it can't hold on any longer! Tap the cloud to let it rain.

💭 This falling water — rain, snow, or hail — is called precipitation. Which of these is also precipitation?

4️⃣ Collection

Rain falls on mountains, fields, and rooftops. It flows downhill, joining little streams, then rivers... and finally, back to the ocean — right where Splash started! 🌊

🖐️ Drag Splash along the river, from the mountain down to the ocean!

🔁 It never stops!

Splash's journey has no beginning and no end — it just keeps going, again and again. This never-ending loop is called the Water Cycle. The same water has been going around and around for millions of years. The water you drink today might once have fallen as rain on a dinosaur! 🦕

🌍 Real-World Water Cycle Mission

Try these at home and tap each one you complete!

  • ☐ Leave a small cup of water on a sunny windowsill. Check it tomorrow — has any disappeared?
  • ☐ Breathe out onto a cold steel spoon or mirror. What do you see appear? (That's condensation!)
  • ☐ Watch the sky for clouds today. Are they fluffy and white, or dark and heavy?
  • ☐ After it rains, look for a puddle. Come back later and see if it's smaller.
