Stage 2 · Discover

Tiny Couriers Everywhere

Every wire is like a tiny street. Living inside it are super-tiny couriers called electrons. When they all rush along the street together in one direction, we call it electric current — and that's electricity!

But couriers only run if their street forms a full loop — with NO gaps. We call that loop a circuit. Ready to build one?

Stage 4 · Sort It

Open Doors & Locked Doors

Conductors are like open doors — electrons rush right through (metals). Insulators are locked doors — electrons can't pass (rubber, wood, plastic, glass).

Tap an object, then tap the correct door.
